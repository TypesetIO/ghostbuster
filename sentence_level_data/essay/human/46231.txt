The differences between shame and guilt have proven to be important in a variety of cases, with these differences becoming important in a variety of contexts that encompass severe mental disorders, our perception of everyday activities, and the variety of topics in-between (Bannister, Colvonen, Angkaw, & Norman, 2019; Parker & Thomas, 2009; Tangney, Stuewig, & Martinez, 2014) .While the overarching differences between shame and guilt are plain, there is a significant gap in the psychological literature concerning how these differences relate to academic motivation.
