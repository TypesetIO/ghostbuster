Even in the years following the Second World War, huge numbers of refugees had been reckoned with, especially since mass refugee movements were part of everyday experience at that time, The same applied for the discussion on the soon-famous right to asylum in West Germany's Basic Law of 1949, which comprised only four words in German: 'Politisch Verfolgte geniefen Asylrecht', 'Persons persecuted on political grounds shall have the right of asylum'.
